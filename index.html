❌ Why Stress Is Bad For Teens

Stress is not just a feeling. It actually does real things to your body!! When you are stressed your body releases a chemical called cortisol. Too much cortisol is really bad for you especially when you are a teenager because your brain is still growing.

⚠️ Here are some of the bad things stress can do to you:

🧠 Your Brain
Stress can mess up your memory and make it hard to focus or think clearly. This is really bad when you have tests!!		 😴 Sleep Problems
When you are stressed it is hard to fall asleep or stay asleep. And teenagers need like 8-10 hours of sleep every night.
🤒 Getting Sick More
Stress makes your immune system weaker so you get colds and get sick way more often than normal.		 😔 Feeling Sad or Anxious
Long term stress can lead to depression and anxiety. These are serious mental health issues that need treatment.
📚 Bad Grades
Ironically stress about school actually makes your grades WORSE because you cant concentrate.		 🤕 Headaches and Stomachaches
A lot of teens get headaches or stomach pain from stress. Your body is basically telling you something is wrong.

 WARNING: If stress goes on for a really long time without being treated it can cause serious mental health problems even when you are an adult. This is why it is SO important to deal with it now!!

 Warning Signs That You Are Too Stressed

Sometimes people don't even realize they are stressed. Here are some signs to look out for:

  •  You feel worried ALL the time and can't stop thinking about things
  •  You get really irritated and angry super easily
  •  You cry a lot more than usual
  •  You can't sleep OR you sleep too much
  •  You stop eating or you eat way too much
  •  You stop caring about school and homework
  •  You avoid hanging out with your friends and family
  •  You have a lot of headaches or stomach aches
  •  You feel like nothing is ever going to get better
Important: If you or a friend are feeling like nothing is going to get better or having really dark thoughts, please tell an adult right away or call/text 988. It is free and confidential.

 How To Help With Stress

The good news is there are lots of ways to manage stress!! You don't have to feel this way forever. Here are some things that actually work (these are backed by science btw):

 1. Exercise!!

Exercise is literally one of the best things you can do for stress. When you exercise your brain releases endorphins which are basically natural happy chemicals. Even just going for a walk for 20 minutes can make you feel SO much better.

 2. Talk to Someone

This one is really important. Talking to a parent, friend, school counselor, or therapist can make you feel way less alone. Sometimes just saying your problems out loud makes them feel smaller.

 3. Put Down Your Phone

I know this one is hard lol but social media actually makes stress worse. Seeing other people's "perfect" lives makes you feel bad about yourself. Try to take breaks from your phone especially before bed.

 4. Get Enough Sleep

Try to go to bed at the same time every night. No screens 30 minutes before bed. Sleep is when your brain heals itself so it is super important.

 5. Write In a Journal

Writing down your feelings can help you understand them better. You don't have to be a good writer. Just write whatever you are thinking or feeling.

 6. Try Deep Breathing

This sounds dumb but it actually works!! Try breathing in for 4 seconds, holding for 4 seconds, then breathing out for 4 seconds. Do this a few times when you feel really stressed.

 My Top Tips List

Here are my personal top tips for dealing with stress (some of these I actually do!!):

✅ Tip 1: Make a to-do list so you don't feel so overwhelmed by everything you have to do
✅ Tip 2: Listen to music that makes you happy
✅ Tip 3: Spend time with your pet if you have one (pets are proven to lower stress!!)
✅ Tip 4: Take breaks when studying. Study for 25 min then take a 5 min break.
✅ Tip 5: Eat breakfast!! Skipping meals makes everything worse.
✅ Tip 6: Remember that its okay to say no sometimes. You don't have to do everything.
✅ Tip 7: Watch something funny. Laughing actually reduces stress hormones!!

📞 Resources and Where to Get Help

If you or someone you know needs help please use these resources:

    • 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline - Call or text 988 anytime 24/7. Free and confidential.

    • Crisis Text Line - Text HOME to 741741. You can chat with a real counselor by text.

    • Teen Mental Health website - www.teenmentalhealth.org - has lots of good info for teens

    • Your school counselor - They are there to help and everything is confidential!!

  • A trusted adult - Parent, aunt/uncle, teacher, coach, etc. Anyone you trust.
REMEMBER: Asking for help is NOT weak. It is actually really brave and smart. Everyone needs help sometimes. 

 Conclusion
